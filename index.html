Introduction to On-the-Job Training (OJT)

On-the-Job Training (OJT) is a practical approach to acquiring new skills and competencies necessary for a specific job or task within the actual working environment. Unlike classroom-based training, OJT is conducted at the workplace, where employees can learn by doing and gain hands-on experience under the guidance of a supervisor or a mentor.

Benefits of On-the-Job Training

  • Immediate application of skills in real work scenarios.
  • Personalized learning experience tailored to the individual's needs.
  • Increased employee engagement and motivation.
  • Cost-effective training method as it utilizes existing resources.
  • Enhanced productivity and efficiency through practical exposure.

Steps in On-the-Job Training

  1. Planning: Identify the skills and knowledge required for the job.
  2. Demonstration: The trainer demonstrates the tasks to the trainee.
  3. Practice: The trainee performs the tasks under supervision.
  4. Feedback: Provide constructive feedback and guidance to improve performance.
  5. Evaluation: Assess the trainee's progress and competency in performing the tasks.

OJT is an effective training method that bridges the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application. It helps employees to become proficient in their roles quickly and efficiently, leading to overall organizational success.
